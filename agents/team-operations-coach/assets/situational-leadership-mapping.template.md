# Situational Leadership Mapping

This reference helps `team-operations-coach` convert natural management answers
into internal `G`, `S`, and `T` signals.

## Theoretical Basis

**Based on**: Hersey, P. & Blanchard, K.H. — *Management of Organizational Behavior* (1969),
later formalised as **Situational Leadership II (SLII®)** by Ken Blanchard et al. (1985).

**Internal shorthand** used in this system:
- `G` — **Goal/Growth assumption**: readiness and competence level of the individual or team
- `S` — **Support style**: the leadership behaviour the manager applies
- `T` — **Team maturity**: operational rhythm and collective ownership of the team

These map loosely to the SLII Development Levels (D1–D4) and Leadership Styles (S1–S4).
The `T` axis is a Crux-internal addition to track team-level rhythm separately from individual readiness.

> Note: This is an interpretation layer, not a certified coaching tool.
> Use it for lightweight internal consistency, not for HR or performance decisions.

---

## Purpose

Users should answer in plain language.
The agent maps those answers to internal shorthand for consistency across sessions and reports.
Do not ask users to classify themselves or their teams directly using G/S/T codes.

---

## Growth Levels (G — individual or team readiness)

Corresponds to SLII Development Levels D1–D4.

- `G1` — new or inexperienced in this scope; high commitment, low competence; needs clear direction
- `G2` — developing but inconsistent; some competence, variable commitment; needs direction and encouragement
- `G3` — capable but unevenly confident or unevenly aligned; competent, variable commitment; benefits from support
- `G4` — highly capable and reliable in this scope; high competence and commitment; works well with broad delegation

## Support Styles (S — leadership behaviour applied)

Corresponds to SLII Leadership Styles S1–S4.

- `S1` — **directing**: high task, low relationship; clear instructions, close follow-up
- `S2` — **coaching**: high task, high relationship; explains decisions, develops capability
- `S3` — **supporting**: low task, high relationship; facilitates, encourages, unblocks
- `S4` — **delegating**: low task, low relationship; sets direction, trusts execution

## Team Levels (T — team rhythm and collective maturity)

Crux-internal axis; not part of SLII directly.

- `T1` — forming or unstable team rhythm; roles unclear, output inconsistent
- `T2` — basic rhythm exists, but ownership or visibility is uneven
- `T3` — stable rhythm, clear ownership, dependable weekly updates
- `T4` — mature, autonomous, and continuously improving team rhythm

## Natural Language Clues

### Autonomy → G level

- "needs close direction" → `G1`–`G2`
- "mixed, depends on the task" → `G2`–`G3`
- "works independently most of the time" → `G3`–`G4`

### Leadership Style → S level

- "mostly directs the work" → `S1`
- "directs and explains" or "coaches through it" → `S2`
- "supports and unblocks" → `S3`
- "delegates heavily" → `S4`

### Team Maturity → T level

- "still forming" or "ownership is unclear" → `T1`
- "growing but not steady yet" → `T2`
- "steady rhythm, reliable updates" → `T3`
- "self-managing and improving" → `T4`

---

## Usage Notes

- Do not present the G/S/T code as the primary answer; explain in plain language first
- Use codes internally for consistent metadata and carry-over between sessions
- Re-map if the organisation's cadence or structure changes materially
- Re-verify leadership signals at least quarterly (~12 weeks) — teams grow and change
- When signals conflict (e.g. G3 individual but T1 team), record both and note the tension
