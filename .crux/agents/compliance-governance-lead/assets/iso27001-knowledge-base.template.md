# ISO 27001 Knowledge Base

> Reusable reference for ISO 27001 consultancy tasks in Crux.
> This is guidance, not legal or certification-body advice.

---

## Core Objective

ISO 27001 work should produce an auditable Information Security Management System (ISMS)
with a clear scope, risk methodology, control selection rationale, evidence ownership,
review cadence, and management oversight.

---

## Core Deliverables

- ISMS scope definition
- organisation context and interested parties
- risk assessment methodology
- risk register and treatment plan
- Statement of Applicability (SoA)
- information security policy set
- supporting procedures and registers
- internal audit and management review plan
- evidence map for certification readiness

---

## Minimum Expectations For Each Document

Every compliance document should state:
- purpose
- scope
- owner
- review frequency
- linked evidence
- related controls or clauses
- assumptions or exclusions

---

## Typical Policy / Procedure Set

- Information Security Policy
- Access Control Policy
- Asset Management Policy
- Supplier Security Policy
- Incident Management Procedure
- Backup and Recovery Procedure
- Change Management Procedure
- Risk Management Methodology
- Business Continuity / Availability Procedure

---

## Common Failure Points

- ISMS scope too broad or too vague
- SoA created without operational control ownership
- risks listed without treatment decisions
- policies written as generic templates with no company context
- missing evidence sources for implemented controls
- supplier and cloud dependencies left out of scope assumptions

---

## Good Working Rule

If a control is claimed as implemented, the deliverable should also identify:
- where the evidence lives
- who owns the control
- how often it is reviewed
- what would prove failure or exception
